About the Fest

Central Level Technical Hackathon

TechNova is the most prestigious inter-college technical festival, bringing together brilliant minds from across universities to solve real-world problems through technology. This year's theme focuses on sustainable innovation for future societies.

With competitions across software, hardware, and emerging technologies, participants will have 36 hours to conceptualize, build, and present their solutions to a panel of industry experts and academic leaders.

General Rules & Regulations

  • All code/hardware must be developed during the hackathon (reusing standard libraries is allowed)
  • Teams must consist of students currently enrolled in any recognized university
  • Decision of judges will be final and binding
  • Code of conduct must be strictly followed - harassment of any kind will not be tolerated

Software Hackathon Details

Problem Statements:

  • Develop an AI-powered tool for early detection of learning disabilities in children
  • Create a blockchain-based agricultural supply chain verification system
  • Build an AR application for heritage site preservation and virtual tours
  • Develop a mental health chatbot with sentiment analysis capabilities

Judging Criteria (100 points):

  • Innovation (30 points)
  • Technical Complexity (25 points)
  • User Experience (20 points)
  • Potential Impact (15 points)
  • Presentation (10 points)

Hardware Hackathon Details

Problem Statements:

  • Design an IoT solution for monitoring air quality in smart cities
  • Create an assistive technology device for differently-abled individuals
  • Build a low-cost water purification monitoring system for rural areas
  • Develop an energy-efficient home automation system

Provided Components:

  • Arduino/Raspberry Pi boards (limited quantities)
  • Basic sensors (temperature, motion, humidity, etc.)
  • Standard electronic components (resistors, capacitors, etc.)
